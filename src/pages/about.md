## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI, also known as embodied intelligence, represents the convergence of artificial intelligence with physical systems that interact with the real world. Unlike traditional AI that exists purely in the digital realm, Physical AI combines:

- **Perception Systems**: Advanced sensors, computer vision, and LIDAR for understanding the environment
- **Decision Making**: Machine learning algorithms that enable autonomous behavior and adaptive responses
- **Physical Embodiment**: Actuators, motors, and mechanical systems that execute actions in the real world
- **Learning Capabilities**: Reinforcement learning and imitation learning that allow robots to improve through experience

Humanoid robotics takes this concept further by creating machines that mimic human form and function. From Boston Dynamics' Atlas performing parkour to Tesla's Optimus handling warehouse tasks, we're witnessing a revolution in how machines understand and interact with human environments. These systems must master complex challenges: maintaining balance on two legs, manipulating objects with dexterous hands, navigating crowded spaces, and understanding human intentionsâ€”all in real-time.

This field sits at the intersection of mechanical engineering, computer science, artificial intelligence, and cognitive science, making it one of the most interdisciplinary and exciting areas of modern technology.
